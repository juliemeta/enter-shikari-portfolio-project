Who is Enter Shikari?

Strong lyrics

In 2003 Enter Shikari was formed in St. Albans, Uk, by members Rou Reynolds (lead vocals and lyricist), Rory Clewlow (guitarist), Rob Rolfe (drummer) and Chris Batten (bassist).
Their songs typically revolve around lyrical themes such as climate change, capitalism, social issues and the like.

"Fear begins to vanish when we realise
That countries are just lines
Drawn in the sand with a stick."

Best live act

One of the reasons fans are dedicated to the band is their performance on stage which is a high-energy explosive experience.
Whether Enter Shikari is performing in a small venue like Pumpehuset in Copenhagen or a big stage like O2 Victoria Warehouse in Manchester they manage to maintain a great connection with the audience. They even performed a sold out show at the Wembley Arena in London.

Eclectic musical universe

Enter Shikari is a rock band combining a bunch of subgenres including electronic, alternative, heavy, punk... you name it! This eclectic combo means that their musical repertoire is always renewing and very diverse. When comparing their debut album "Take to the Skies" from 2007 and one of their newer releases like "Nothing is True and Everything is Possible", it becomes clear just how diverse their musical talent is.
Listen for yourself and try to decipher all the genres you think define their music.

Recommendations

Lyrics

Would you like to dive into Enter Shikari's lyrical universe, a personal recommendation is to get a copy of the book Dear Future Historians.
The book contains the lyrics to their songs from 2006-2019 with explanatory comments from author Rou Reynolds who is the band's lyricist and lead singer.

Shikari Sound System

Enter Shikari have a side project named Shikari Sound System. When they perform DJ sets under this alter ego, they embrace their electronic musical talent that goes beyond their usual electronicore sound.

I listened to Enter Shikari's debut album in elementary school and wasn't familiar with any of their other releases from back then (around 2009) up until a decade later in 2018 when I sort of randomly ended up at a concert with the band - and I've been hooked ever since.
So far, I've been to four gigs, with the biggest show being in Manchester. It was such a different experience seeing the band at a big venue in their homeland compared to the other three times where I have seen them in smaller music venues in Denmark where I live.
